Entre - Chicken Piccata

We're all hungry enough, let's start eating already. Here's a quick 30 minute recipe for an amazing chicken piccata with pasta.

Ingredients

  • 4 skinless, boneless chicken breast halves or chicken thights
  • cayenne pepper
  • salt and black pepper
  • all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon capers
  • 1/2 cup white wine (hopefully there's still some left)
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoon parsley

Directions

  1. Place chicken between 2 sheets of heavy pastic on a solid surface. Firmly pound chicken with the smooth side of a meat malet to a 1/2 inch thickness.
  2. Season both sides of chicken breast with cayenne, salt, black pepper, and whatever else you want to through on there (Let the wine speak to you)Then, dredge lightly with flour and shake off any excess.
  3. Start by salting water in a pot and bringing it to a boil. When ready, add pasta of your choice for 10-12 minutes.
  4. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Place chicken in the pan and reduce heat, cook until browned and cooked throughout, about 5 minutes per side,
  5. Cook capers in reserved oil, smashing them lightly to release brine, for about 30 seconds. Then, add white wine and bring to a boil while scraping all those delicious browned bits off the bottom of the pan. Cook until half reduced, about 2 minutes.
  6. Stir in lemon juice, water, and butter. Cook and stir continuosly to form a thick sauce, about 2 minutes. Reduce heat to low and stor parsley through the sauce.
  7. Return chicken to the pan and cook until heated, 1-2 minutes.
  8. Ensure pasta is cooked and strained, then add butter or olive oil to taste,
  9. Serve with sauce spooned on top and pasta underneath or on the side.

Voila! A super easy and delicous meal. I hope you saved room because after this we have dessert!
